# Unified Leveling Standard (단일 레벨 기준표)

This table is the single source of truth. It reconciles the legacy documents
(00 지침 vs 01 기사 프롬프트), which disagreed on Times levels.

## NE Times (최상위, middle–high school)

| Internal level | Operational CEFR | Easier variant | Harder variant | Typical pages & word counts |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | B1 | A2-B1 | B1-B2 | 3-1면 190–200w · 3-2면 ~100w · 5면 135–140w · Briefs 65–77w |
| Level 2 | B2 | B1-B2 | B2-C1 | 2면 270–280w (no subheading) · 8면 280–290w (2 subheadings) · 12면 280–290w (3 subheadings) |
| Level 3 | C1 (operational) | B2-C1 | — (do not exceed C1) | 4면 ~300w (no subheading) |

> Note: the legacy 레벨링 표 lists L3 as "C2", but every actual production
> prompt specifies C1, and C2 is pedagogically inappropriate for this
> readership. Use C1.

| CEFR | Notes |

## Level-writing guidance

- **A1 / A1-A2**: 2-sentence paragraphs, high-frequency vocabulary, simple
  present/past, no subordinate-clause chains.
- **A2-B1 / B1 (Times L1)**: short paragraphs, one idea each; limited
  passive voice; explain any technical term in-line.
- **B2 (Times L2)**: natural connectors, some idiomatic phrasing, varied
  sentence openings; still avoid academic register.
- **C1 (Times L3)**: complex clauses and abstract vocabulary allowed; keep
  the structure transparent — difficulty should come from language, not from
  disorganized content.
- When a request says "더 쉽게/더 어렵게", shift one column in the table —
  rewrite from scratch at the new level rather than lightly editing, unless
  the user asks for a light edit.
